{% raw %}CHAPTER X.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A TEEEIBLE TEMPTATION.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}SIGH FOR DEATH.—A MURDER IN MY HEART.—THE AXE RAISED.—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}CONSCIENCE SPEAKS AND I AM SAVED.—GOD BE rRAISED !{% endraw %}
{% raw %}All outward nature seemed to feed my gloomy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thoughts. I know not what most men see in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}voyaging down the Mississippi. If gay and hopeful,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probably much of beauty and interest. If eager{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merchants, probably a golden river, freighted with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wealth of nations. I saw nothing but portents{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of woe and despair. Wretched slave-pens ; a smell{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of stagnant waters ; half-putrid carcases of horses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or oxen floating along, covered with turkey-buzzards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and swarms of green flies,—these are the images{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with which memory crowds my mind. My faith in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}God utterly gave way. I could no longer pray of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trust. I thought He had abandoned me and cast me{% endraw %}
{% raw %}off for ever. I looked not to Him for help. I saw{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only the foul miasmas, the emaciated frames of my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}negro companions ; and in them saw the sure, swift,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}loving intervention of the one unfailing friend of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wretched,—death ! Yes ; death and the grave !{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" There the wicked cease from troubling, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}weary are at rest. There the prisoners rest together ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they hear not the voice of the oppressor." Two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}years of this would kill me. I dwelt on the thought{% endraw %}
