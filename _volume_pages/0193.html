{% raw %}It seemed to me if I took some of the apples that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fell from the trees in the orchard and sold them I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should be able to get the money for the spelling-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}book. I did this. Early the next morning I was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about to harness the horse for my master; the horse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was frisky and ran, and I ran to catch him, when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}my hat fell off and the book in it dropped on to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground. After I had harnessed the horse my master{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exclaimed, "What's that?" "A spelling-book."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Whose is it?" "Mine." "Where did you get{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it?" "Bought it, sir, when I went to market."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"How much was it?" "Eleven cents." "Where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did you get the money ?" " I sold some apples out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of our orchard." " Our orchard !" he exclaimed, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a passion. "I'll teach you to get apples from our{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orchard for such a vile purpose, so you'll remember{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it. Give me that book." I stooped to pick it up,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and as I saw his big cane coming down I dodged.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Pick up that book," he cried, using an awful oath.{% endraw %}
