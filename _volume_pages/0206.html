{% raw %}202 MRS. H. BEECHER STOWE's " UNCLE TOM."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Memorial Church Rectory, London, Ontario,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}May 16, 1876.—To the Secretaries of the Colonial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Continental Church Society.—Dear Sirs,—In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the last report to the Society, our lamented friend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mr. Hughes speaks of a proposed visit to England{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Rev. Josiah Henson (Mrs. Stowe's ' Uncle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tom') for the purpose of raising funds to clear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}off a mortgage which Mr. Henson had to give on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his farm in order to meet the costs of a lawsuit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the Dawn Institute. The object of this note is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to introduce him to you, hoping that you may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able to further his cause in England. He was well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known'to the friends of the coloured race twenty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}years ago, but the changes make it essential that he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should have some who can recommend him in his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present effort. You are already acquainted with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}work of the Wilberforce Institute, which has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sustained at great personal expense by Mr. Henson.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}His experience as a slave, and as a preacher among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fugitives in Canada, makes his story extremely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting. Mrs. Stowe, in her 'Key to Uncle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tom's Cabin,' gives a sketch of his life to confirm{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the character she has painted. On the authority of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mr. Hughes, who knew Mr. Henson for many years,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and thought most highly of his work and character,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I beg to introduce to you one who has been a great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blessing to his coloured brethren in Canada. If you{% endraw %}
{% raw %}can give him any letters, or further his cause in any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way, it will assist the movement with which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Colonial and Continental Church Society has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connected for many years.—Yours very sincerely,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. Harrison Tilley, Clerical Secretary to Corre-{% endraw %}
