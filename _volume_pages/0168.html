{% raw %}Difficulties had arisen in the management of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Manual Labour Institution at Dawn,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before I visited England in 1851. Debts had ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cumulated, and I had pledged myself to take the saw¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mill and part of the land, and clear off the debts as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I have before explained. The school was established{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the express idea that it was not to promulgate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sectarianism. But those who had obtained control{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over it, were inclined to drift it into a particular sect.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I opposed this, and hence incurred their disapproba¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion. Soon after I visited England, and began to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}raise a fund for the benefit of the school; but, as I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before stated, the dominant party attempted to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}counteract my efforts by slandering me. My com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mittee in London proposed that a gentleman should{% endraw %}
