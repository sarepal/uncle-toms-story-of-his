{% raw %}When the above attack was made upon me, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meeting of those interested in my cause was called,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and my accuser, who was in the country, was re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quested to meet me face to face.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I believe all the difficulty arose from little petty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jealousies, fostered, perhaps, by the unworthy influ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ences of slavery, over the misguided people who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were for a time misled by false representations.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We met before a company of English gentlemen,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who heard all that my accuser had to say. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}asked me for a reply. I simply restated to them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the facts I had previously made known. I reminded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them that a man who devotes himself to doing good,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must and will be misunderstood and have enemies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I called their attention to the misinterpretation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their own motives made by their enemies. I related{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to them the parable of Christ about the wheat and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the tares. My recommendatory letters were re-read{% endraw %}
{% raw %}—a sufficient reply to the allegation that I was an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}impostor.{% endraw %}
