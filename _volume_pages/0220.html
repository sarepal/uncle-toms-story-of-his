{% raw %}APPENDIX.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Notwithstanding all that has been done, the African slave-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trade as a whole, is, at this moment, probably as great as it has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been at any previous time."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"The slave-trade has now existed more than three centuries,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and within that period, according to a careful French writer,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more than fifty millions of slaves have been taken from Africa."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" The traffic mostly now carried on by the overland routes east¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ward has enormously increased. The principal countries on behalf{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which the present African slave-trade is carried on are Turkev,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt, Persia, Tunis, Morocco, and Madagascar. On them the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}responsibility for the present state of Africa now mainly rests."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Sir Bartle Frere, in the Blue Book presented to the Houses of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parliament in 1873, states that, ' The correspondence of the Central{% endraw %}
{% raw %}African Vicariate Apostolic extends over countries roughly esti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mated at having a population of 80,000,000 of negroes, between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Red and Arabian Seas on the east, and the Atlantic on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}west ; and the annual drain consequent on slavery is estimated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Superior of the Mission at 1,000,000.' Dr. Livingstone-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}calculated that not more than one slave in five arrived at his desti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nation, and on some routes not one in nine. This does not include{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the loss of life caused by the torture of boys for the markets of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egypt and Turkey, under which two out of every three perish."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" In all the expeditions it should be borne in mind that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cause of the natives is never heard. We only hear the statements{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Europeans who enter into these engagements, and they go so{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equipped and armed that, it has been forcibly remarked, their lives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are insured. When any great amount of slaughter has been com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mitted the aggressors congratulate one another on their bravery and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gallant bearing, and the world applauds."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'' At the present moment England, France, and America may,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a certain sense, be said to patronise slavery in the East. Their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Consuls in those countries appoint agents in the principal towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and centres who are supporters of slavery and owners of slaves.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Over the roofs of their houses wave the flags of Christian nations,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and under them are the slaves of these Consular Agents."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"The following remarks are taken from a very important paper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}written in Egypt, by Sir Bartle Frere, on his route to Zanzibar :—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' It can hardly escape so enlightened a ruler as His Highness that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slavery is in itself a canker which must eat into the vitals of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}country like Egypt, whose prosperity depends in so large a degree{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the industry of the agricultural class. . . His Highness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expressed a hope that the stoppage of the supply of slaves from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interior would ultimately tend towards a gradual diminution and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}final extinction of slavery in Egypt. 1 feel that all experience in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against this eeepectation. Whilst the elemand continues I believe it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be practically impossible to cut off the supply. This is especially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the case where the sources of supply are so many and spread over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so large an area that ages would hardly suffice to reach them all by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}separate measures of repression. But if the demand is extinguished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the object is at once effected and the trade must cease."{% endraw %}
