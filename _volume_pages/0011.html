{% raw %}rpHE numerous friends of the author of this work{% endraw %}
{% raw %}-^ will need no greater recommendation than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his name to make it welcome. Among all the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}singular and interesting records to which the insti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tution of American slavery has given rise, we know{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of none more striking, more characteristic and in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structive, than that of Josiah Henson.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Born a %lave—a slave in effect in a heathen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land—and under a heathen master, he grew up{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without Christian light or knowledge, and like the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gentiles spoken of by St. Paul, " without the law{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did by nature the things that are written in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}law." One sermon, one offer of salvation by Christ,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was sufficient for him, as for the Ethiopian eunuch,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to make him at once a believer from the heart and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a preacher of Jesus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the great Christian doctrine of forgiveness of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enemies and the returning of good for evil, he was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by God's grace made a faithful witness, under cir¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cumstances that try men's souls and make us all who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}read it say, "Lead us not into such temptation."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We earnestly commend this portion of his narrative{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to those who, under much smaller temptations, think{% endraw %}
{% raw %}themselves entitled to render evil for evil.{% endraw %}
