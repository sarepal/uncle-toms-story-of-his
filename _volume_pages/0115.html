{% raw %}I remained at home, working on my farm, until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the next autumn, about the time I had pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mised to assist in the restoring to liberty the friends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of James Lightfoot, the individual who had excited{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mv sympathy at the meeting at Fort Erie. In pur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suance of this promise, I again started on my long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}journey into Kentucky.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On my way, that strange occurrence happened,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called the great meteoric shower. The heavens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seemed broken up into streaks of light and falling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stars. I reached Lancaster, Ohio, at three o'clock{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the morning, found the village aroused, the bells{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tinging, and the people exclaiming, " The day of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}judgment is come ! " I thought it was probably so;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but felt that I was in the right business, and walked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on through the village, leaving the terrified people{% endraw %}
{% raw %}behind The stars continued to fall till the light of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sun appeared.{% endraw %}
