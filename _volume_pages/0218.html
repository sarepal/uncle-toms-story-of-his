{% raw %}APPENDIX.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between 1835 and 1847, Cincinnati became the prominent battle¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground of freedom and slavery. It will now be clear to the reader{% endraw %}
{% raw %}how painfully familiar Mrs. H. B. Stowe became with the horrors{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of slavery. The road which ran through Walnut Hills, only a few{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feet from Mrs. Stowe's door, was ultimately a favourite route of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"The Underground Railway," so called, and so familiar in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pages of "Uncle Tom's Cabin." The "railway" consisted of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}noble line of Quakers and other anti-slavery friends, who lived at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intervals, say, of fifteen or twenty miles, between the Ohio River{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Northern Lakes. These friends had combined to help{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fugitive slaves forward in their escape to Canada. A fugitive would{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be taken at night, on horseback or in a covered waggon, from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station to station, as described, until he stood on a free soil, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found the British banner floating o'er his head. Or,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" Thus when her form flits wildly by,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}With bloodless cheek and fearless eye,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Resolved to free her child or die,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We still our very breath—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Till, safely on the farther shore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}She stands, the desperate journey o'er{% endraw %}
{% raw %}So fraught with life and death."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Referring once more to the " Underground Railway," we may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remind our readers that the first station north of Cincinnati was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}few miles up Mill Creek, at the house of the pious and lion-hearted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vanzant, otherwise called Van Trompe in " Uncle Tom's Cabin."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Such being the roadway, Mrs. Stowe would inevitably be roused,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and frequently, by the rapid rattle of the covered waggons, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the noisy galloping of the horses ridden by the constables and slave-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}catchers, who would be in hot pursuit as they madly passed her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door. Vanzant (the "Honest John," as he was called) was always{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ready to turn out with his team, and the hunters were rarely clever{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enough to come up with him. He has long since filled a martyr's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grave. Mrs. Stowe, therefore, during her long residence on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frontier of the slave states, by several visits to them, would natu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rally become familiar in observations of them, and furnish herself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with ample material for her masterpiece on slavery. We cannot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refuse one of Mrs. Stowe's sketches in 1840. " The slave-catchers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}backed by the riff-raff of the population, and urged on by certain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}politicians and merchants, attacked the quarters in which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}negroes reside. Some of the houses were battered down by cannon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}For several days the city was abandoned to violence and crime.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The negro-quarters were pillaged and sacked. Negroes attempting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to defend their property were killed, and their bodies thrown into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the streets. Women cruelly injured by ruffians, some afterwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dying of their injuries. Houses were burnt, and men, women, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}children were betrayed in the confusion, and hurried into slavery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the brow of the hill on which I lived, I could hear the cries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the victims, the shouts of the mob, the reports of guns and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cannon, and could see the flames of the conflagration. To more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than one of the trembling fugitives I have given shelter, and wept{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bitter tears with them. After the fury of the mob was spent,{% endraw %}
