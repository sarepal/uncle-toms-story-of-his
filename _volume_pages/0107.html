{% raw %}CHAPTER XV{% endraw %}
{% raw %}LIFE TX CANADA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}CONDITION OF THE BLACKS IN CANADA.—A TOUR OF EXPLORATION..{% endraw %}
{% raw %}—APPEAL TO THE LEGISLATURE.—IMPROVEMENTS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After about three years had passed, I improved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}my condition again by taking service with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gentleman by the name of Riseley, whose residence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was only a few miles distant. He was a man of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more elevation of mind than Mr. Hibbard, and of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}superior abilities. At his place I began to reflect,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more and more, upon the circumstances of the-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blacks, who were already somewhat numerous in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this region. I was not the only one who had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}escaped from the States, and had settled on the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spot in Canada which they had reached. Several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hundreds of coloured persons were in the neighbour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hood, and, in the first joy of their deliverance, they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were living in a way, which, I could see, led to little{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or no progress in improvement. They were content{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to have the proceeds of their labour at their own{% endraw %}
{% raw %}command, and had not the ambition for, or the per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ception of what was within their easy reach, if they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}did. but know it. They were generally working for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hire upon the lands of others, and had not yet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dreamed of becoming independent proprietors them¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}selves. It soon became my great object to awaken{% endraw %}
