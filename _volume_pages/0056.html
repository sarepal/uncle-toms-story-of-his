{% raw %}As I surveyed this scene, and listened to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groans and outcries of my afflicted companions, my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eyes were opened, and I lamented that I had pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vented them from availing themselves of the oppor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunity for acquiring freedom which offered itself at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cincinnati. I had only thought of being faithful to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}my master's interests, and nothing of the welfare of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the slaves. Oh! what would I not have given to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have had the chance offered once more! But now,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through me, were they doomed to wear out life{% endraw %}
{% raw %}miserably in the hot and pestilential climate of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}far south. Death would have been welcome to me{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in my agony. From that hour I saw through,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hated, and cursed the whole system of slavery. One{% endraw %}
{% raw %}absorbing purpose occupied my soul—to gain free¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dom, self-assertion, and deliverance from the cruel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}caprices and fortunes of dissolute tyrants. Once to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}get away, with my wife and children, to some spot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where I could feel that they were indeed mine—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where no grasping master could stand between me{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and them, as arbiter of their destiny—was a heaven{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yearned after with insatiable longing. For it I stood{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ready to pray, toil, dissemble, plot like a fox, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fight like a tiger. All the noble instincts of my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soul, and all the ferocious passions of my animal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nature, were aroused and quickened into vigorous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}action.{% endraw %}
