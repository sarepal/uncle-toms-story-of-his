{% raw %}During the bright and hopeful days I spent in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ohio, while away on my preaching tour, I had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heard much of the course pursued by fugitives from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slavery, and became acquainted with a number of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}benevolent men engaged in helping them on their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way. Canada was often spoken of as the only sure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refuge from pursuit, and that blessed land was now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the desire of my longing heart. Infinite toils and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perils lay between me and that haven of promise,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enough to daunt the stoutest heart; but the fire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}behind me was too hot and fierce to let me pause to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consider them. I knew the North Star—blessed be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}God for setting it in the heavens ! Like the Star of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bethlehem, it announced where my salvation lay.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}field, it would guide my feet in the way of hope. I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thought of it as my God-given guide to the land of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promise far away beneath its light. I knew that it{% endraw %}
