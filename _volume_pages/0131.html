{% raw %}The land on which we settled in Canada was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered with a beautiful forest of noble tree:*{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of various kinds. Our people were accustomed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cut them down and burn them on the ground, simply{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to get rid of them. Often as I roamed through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forest, I was afflicted at seeing such waste, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longed to devise some means of converting this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}abundant natural wealth into money, so as to im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prove the condition of the people.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Full of this subject, I left my home on a journey{% endraw %}
{% raw %}-of observation through the State of New York, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}New England. I kept my purposes to myself, not{% endraw %}

{% raw %}found in New York, mills where precisely such logs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}-as those in Canada were sawed into lumber, which I{% endraw %}
