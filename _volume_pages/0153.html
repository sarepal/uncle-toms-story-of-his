{% raw %}She was rejoiced to see me once more, while at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same time she said that perhaps she had done wrong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in allowing me to leave England when my business-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prospects were so flattering. I told her that I was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more than satisfied, that I was truly thankful to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}my Heavenly Father for granting us this interview,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}no matter what the pecuniary sacrifice might be.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We talked over our whole past life as far as her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}strength would permit, reviewing the many scenes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of sorrow and trouble, as well as the many bright{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and happy days of our pilgrimage, until exhausted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nature sought repose, and she sunk into a quiet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sleep.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The day following she revived ; my return seemed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to inspire her with the hope that possibly she might{% endraw %}
{% raw %}j:gain be restored to health. It was not, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so to be; but God in His mercy granted her a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reprieve, and her life was prolonged a few weeks.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I thus had the melancholy satisfaction of watching{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day and night by her bed of languishing and pain,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was permitted to close her eyes when the final{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summons came. She blessed me, and blessed her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}children, commending us to the ever-watchful care{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of that Saviour who had sustained her in so many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bours of trial; and finally, after kissing me and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each one of the children, she passed from earth to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heaven without a pang or a groan, as gently as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}falling to sleep of an infant on its mother's breast.{% endraw %}
