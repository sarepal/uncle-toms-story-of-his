{% raw %}"Dresden, Ontario, Canada, March 10, 1876.—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mr. Josiah Henson being about to proceed to Eng¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land, has requested me to give him a letter testi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monial. Mr. Henson is so highly respected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}throughout Western Canada, and also so well known{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to many influential persons, both in the United{% endraw %}
{% raw %}States and in England, that he scarcely needs any¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thing of the kind from any individual. I have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known Mr. Henson for more than sixteen years, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have great pleasure in bearing my testimony to his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sterling Christian character. Mr. Henson's life has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been an unusually active and eventful one. For{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many years he was a slave, and was most cruelly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}treated; and since his escape to Canada, now more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than forty years ago, he has occupied a foremost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place in all movements for the advancement of his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people. Through his efforts for their good he has,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unfortunately, suffered considerable pecuniary loss,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and has been compelled in consequence to mortgage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his farm. It is with the view of lifting this in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cumbrance that he has, in his extreme old age,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resolved, in response to a cordial invitation given{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him, to visit England. I heartily commend him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and his cause to the British public, and hope that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he may have, in every respect, a 'prosperous journey{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the will of God.'—Thos. Hughes, Missionary of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Church of England, Dresden."{% endraw %}
