{% raw %}The dinner at Lord John Russell's, as detailed in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the previous chapter, was in the month of June,.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1852; from that time to the 1st of August, I was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}busily employed in finishing up all matters connected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with my agency, in which I was very successful^{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having accomplished the objects of my mission..{% endraw %}
{% raw %}During the month of August, I was engaged in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}publishing a narrative of incidents in my slave-life,.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which I had been urgently requested to do by some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the noblest men and women in England. Just as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I had completed the work, I received, on the 3rd{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of September, a letter from my family in Canada,.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}life, the sharer of my joys and sorrows, was at the-{% endraw %}

{% raw %}return immediately, that she might see me onco{% endraw %}
