{% raw %}CHAPTER IV{% endraw %}
{% raw %}MY CONVEESIOX{% endraw %}
{% raw %}MY PRAYING MOTHER.—A GOOD MAN.—HEAR A SERMON FOR THE{% endraw %}
{% raw %}FIRST TIME.— ITS EFFECT UPON ME.—PRAYER AND COMMUNION.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}—ITS FIRST FRUITS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I remember being torn from a dear and affec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tionate mother; I saw her tears and heard her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groans ; I remember all the particulars. From a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little boy up I have remembered my mother; I re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}member what the prayers of my dear mother were;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I have heard her pray for me; for she was a good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christian woman before I was born ; and I thank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}God that I was born of a good Christian mother, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mother whose prayers fell on my ear. Of all earthly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blessings there is none can approach to a good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mother. I remember her entreaties; I remember{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her prayers to God for me. Blessed is the child,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the son or daughter, that has the prayers of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mother. I remember well the feeling that those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prayers wrought upon my heart, though I was but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a boy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}My heart exults with gratitude when I mention the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of a good man who first taught me the blessed¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ness of religion. His name was John McKenny.{% endraw %}
