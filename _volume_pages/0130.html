{% raw %}126 MRS. H. BEECHER STOWE's " UNCLE TOM."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}My efforts to aid them, in every way in my power,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and to procure the aid of others for them, have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constant. I have made many journeys into New{% endraw %}
{% raw %}York, Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Maine, in all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which States I have found or made many friends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the cause, as well as personal friends. I have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}received many liberal gifts for my people, and ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perienced much kindness of treatment; but I must{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be allowed to allude particularly to the donations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leceived from Boston—by which we were enabled to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erect a sawmill, and thus to begin in good earnest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the clearing of our lands, and to secure a profitable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}return for the support of our school—as among those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which have been most welcome and valuable to us.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Some of the trips I have made, have led to some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}incidents and observations which must be the theme{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a future chapter.{% endraw %}
