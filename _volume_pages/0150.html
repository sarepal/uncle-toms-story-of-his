{% raw %}146 MRS. H. BEECHER STOWE's " UNCLE TOM."{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cheers and laughter followed the reading of this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}toast, succeeded by the usual English exclamations,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" Up, up, up again /" I again arose and gave, To>{% endraw %}
{% raw %}our most Sovereign Lady, the Queen:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"May she have a long life, and a happy death. May she reign{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in righteousness, and rule in love ! "{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And to her illustrious consort, Prince Albert:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}" May he have peace at home, pleasure abroad, love his Queen,.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and serve the Lord!"{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Among the distinguished persons who made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}speeches on this joyous occasion, were Rev. William{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Brock, Hon. Samuel M. Peto, and the brother-in-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}law of Mr. Peto, with his accomplished and beautiful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lady. Thus ended one of the pleasantest days of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}my life.{% endraw %}
