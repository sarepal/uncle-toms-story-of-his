{% raw %}MY VISIT TO MRS. STOWE.—WHY I AM CALLED " UNCLE TOM.""{% endraw %}
{% raw %}—HER INTEREST IN MY LIFE-STORY.—HER. FAMOUS BOOK.—Is.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}IT AN EXAGGERATION?—MRS. STOWE'S KEY.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After my successful visit to England, I travelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Canada, and in Maine, New Hampshire,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vermont, Massachusetts, Connecticut, and Rhode{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Island. In all these states I was cordially welcomed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a speaker in the pulpits of the Congregationalists,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Presbyterians, Methodists, Baptists, and Universal-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ists. I held many meetings, and discussed the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subject of slavery in all its bearings on society. At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that time, slavery was considered to be a permanent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}institution of the South, and it was supposed that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nothing but an earthquake would have the power to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}break up the foundations of the system. It is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mistaken idea that the majority of the slaveholders{% endraw %}
{% raw %}would have sold their slaves if the government had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offered to buy them. They liked the system, had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grown up with it, and were not disposed to part{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with it without a struggle. Anti-slavery ideas were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not popular at the South, nor generally at the North.{% endraw %}
