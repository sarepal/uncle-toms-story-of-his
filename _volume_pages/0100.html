{% raw %}NEW SCEXES AXD A NEW HOME.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A POOR MAN IN A STRANGE LAND.—EEGIN TO ACQUIRE PROPERTY..{% endraw %}
{% raw %}—RESUME PREACHING.—BOYS GO TO SCHOOL.—WHAT GAVE ME A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}DESIRE TO LEARN TO READ.—A DAY OF PRAYER IN THE WOODS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There was not much time to be lost, though, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frolic even, at this extraordinary moment. I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was a stranger in a strange land, and had to look{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about me at once for refuge and resource. I found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a lodging for the night, and the next morning set{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about exploring the interior for the means of sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}port. I knew nothing about the country or the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people, but kept my eyes and ears open, and made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such inquiries as opportunity afforded. I heard, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course of the day, of a Mr. Hibbard, who lived some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}six or seven miles off. He was a rich man, as riches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were counted there, had a large farm, and several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small tenements on it, which he was in the habit of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}letting to his labourers. To him I went immediately,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}though the character given him by his neighbours{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was not, by any means, unexceptionably good. But{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I thought he was not, probably, any worse than those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I had been accustomed to serve, and that I could get{% endraw %}
{% raw %}along with him, if honest and faithful work would{% endraw %}
{% raw %}satisfy him. In the afternoon I found him, and soon{% endraw %}
