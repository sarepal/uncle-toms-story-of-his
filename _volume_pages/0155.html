{% raw %}MY BROTHER'S FREEDOM.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}I received numerous tokens of regard from many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}philanthropic gentlemen while I was in Lon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}don, which I shall never forget; but I was particu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}larly touched by the special kindness of Samuel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Morley, Esq., and George Hitchcock, Esq., of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paul's Churchyard These two gentlemen invited{% endraw %}
{% raw %}me to dine with them every day at half-past one.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I gratefully accepted their invitation, and dined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alternately with these gentlemen, always receiving{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a very warm welcome from them. The spirit of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}manhood, one of the strongest elements of my mind,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}received and entertained as a respected guest. One{% endraw %}

{% raw %}past. I remembered the trying scenes of my event¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful life, and that my only brother was still bound in{% endraw %}
