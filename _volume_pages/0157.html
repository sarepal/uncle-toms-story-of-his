{% raw %}found my brother's mind so demoralised or stultified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by slavery, that he would not risk his Life in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attempt to gain his freedom, and he informed me-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of this fact. Still I could not rest contented, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mr. Chaplain promised to make another effort, as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he intended to visit the neighbourhood again. He{% endraw %}
{% raw %}laboured with my brother the second time, with no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}good result, and then he endeavoured to assist Mr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Toomb's slaves, who had resolved to escape from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Georgia to Canada. Mr. Chaplain was detected,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and thrown into prison to await a trial. He wa&{% endraw %}
{% raw %}released on bail, three times the amount of the value{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the slaves. The Hathaways, benevolent Quakers of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farmington, Xew York, Asa B. Smith, and William{% endraw %}
{% raw %}R. Smith, his son, of the same town, paid the bail,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which they desired Mr. Chaplain to forfeit, as they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}knew that the result of a trial would be that he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}would be hung. I will here add that the Smiths{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had to sell their farms, and were pecuniarily ruined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the time, and it is with pleasure that I make{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this record of their generosity in the Anti-Slavery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cause.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On my return to Canada, the release of my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brother was my uppermost thought. Whenever{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I have adopted the language of the prodigal son,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who said, "I will arise, and go to my father,"{% endraw %}
{% raw %}—that is, when I have uttered in my heart the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}words, " God helping me, I will,"—I have some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}how had the ability to accomplish my undertaking.{% endraw %}
